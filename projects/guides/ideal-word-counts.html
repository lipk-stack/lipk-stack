Ideal word counts for every type of writing

A quick, practical reference for how long things should be — with the platform character limits that actually get posts rejected. Need to check as you write? Use the free Word & Character Counter (runs in your browser, nothing uploaded).

Academic & professional writing

  • High-school essay: 300–800 words (about 1–3 pages).
  • College essay / term paper: 1,500–5,000 words depending on the brief.
  • College application essay: 250–650 words (the Common App caps at 650).
  • Cover letter: 250–400 words — half a page, three or four short paragraphs.
  • Résumé / CV: 400–800 words; one page early-career, two pages max.
  • Abstract: 150–300 words.

Blogging & content marketing

  • Standard blog post: 1,000–1,500 words.
  • In-depth / pillar guide: 2,000–3,000+ words for competitive topics.
  • Meta description: aim for under ~155 characters or Google truncates it.
  • Title tag: roughly 50–60 characters before it gets cut off in search results.
  • Email subject line: 30–50 characters reads well on mobile.

Writing for search? The counter also shows keyword density so you can check your main term isn't over- or under-used.

Social media character limits

  • X / Twitter post: 280 characters (free accounts).
  • Instagram caption: up to 2,200 characters; first ~125 show before "more".
  • LinkedIn post: 3,000 characters; ~210 show before "see more".
  • Facebook post: very long limit, but ~80 characters gets the most engagement.
  • YouTube title: 100 characters max; ~70 visible in most layouts.
  • SMS: 160 characters per single message segment.

How long will it take to read or say?

A useful rule of thumb: people read at about 200–250 words per minute and speak at about 130–150. So a 5-minute talk is roughly 650–750 spoken words, and a 1,500-word article takes about 6–7 minutes to read. The word counter estimates both for any text you paste.
